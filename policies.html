College Policies

  1. Requirement for retention

    • Minimum Grade Requirement
      • The minimum grade requirement for a student to be able to proceed to the next professional course and/or semester for BSCS, BSIT and BSIS is 1.0 while for BLIS is 2.0.

    • Cumulative Quality Point Average (CQPA)
      • The student needs to maintain a CQPA of 2.0 to remain in the program and to graduate and earn the degree. CQPA is to be calculated every semester.

    • Professional Courses
      • A student who fails a professional courses twice will be advised to shift to:
        • other programs of the College if the major course he failed in is exclusive to that program/degree;
        • other colleges if the major course he failed in is common to all other programs/degrees offered by the College.

  2. On Related Matters
     
    1. On probation
      • If the student's CQPA falls below 2.0, he/she will be placed in the “On Probation” status watch list. The student can only be in OP status for two (2) consecutive semesters. After the two (2) consecutive semesters, the student will be advised to shift to another degree.
         
    2. Remedial Courses
      • If the student's CQPA falls below 2.0, the student is required to enroll remedial courses to improve his/her CQPA. Upon evaluation and recommendation from the Department Chairperson and approval of the Dean, the student is required to take in the current semester remedial course/s to increase the CQPA. The total number of units must not to exceed the allowable maximum load in that semester. Remedial courses can be new courses, or the course/s with the lowest grade.
         
    3. Removal Exam
      • Faculty will not give removal exams.
         
  3. Requirement for Shifting within CCS
     
    • Automatic shifting is not allowed. A CCS student who wants to shift to another degree offered by the College must get an approval from the Department Chairperson of the program where she/he wants to shift.
       
  4. Requirements for Cross-Enrollment
     
    • Any student from other units of the University who cross-enrolls in any of the professional or elective course of the College must satisfy all the prerequisite requirements of the course;
       
    • CCS students are not allowed to take professional or elective courses outside the College/University.
       
  5. Policy for Crediting Non-Professional Courses
     
    • Only the Dean and any of the Department Chairpersons can sign the Advanced Credit Form.
       
    • Students coming from non-accredited schools/universities (COE/COD in ITE, PAASCU, ACSCU, PICAB) need to retake the computer course.
       
    • Students coming from COE/COD in ITE, PAASCU, ACSCU, or PICAB accredited schools/universities shall present a copy of their transcript of records (TOR) and a description of the course (or courses) taken at their former schools.

  2. On Examination
     
    • All students are expected to take the preliminary, mid-term and final examinations at the time scheduled by the office of the Dean of the concerned Department.   3. On Cheating
     
    • Per faculty action, June 3, 1987:
       
    • Any student caught cheating in a major exam (prelim, mid-term, and final) will be given an "F" for the course; and for other exams (chapter, quizzes), an "F" for such particular exam or quiz.
       
  4. On Class Attendance
     
    • Students are expected to attend all classes. Three (3) unexcused absences may be incurred. The rest of the 20% allowed by CHED must be excused, and incurred only for valid reasons.
       
  5. Students Rights and Responsibilities
     
    1. Students and student organizations enjoy the right to freely examine and discuss all issues of interest and to express opinions privately or publicly.
       
    2. It is the responsibility of all students to observe all duly approved University policies, rules and regulations, and those of the college and their respective Departments. Violations are a cause for sanctions.
       
    3. The university shall handle disciplinary cases in a fair and most expeditious manner possible.
       
    4. The authority to impose disciplinary measures involving suspension, dismissal, and expulsion upon the recommendation of the University Disciplinary Board (UDB) lies on the University President.

      1. Looking at another student's answers during examinations;

    3. Violations of academic conduct also relate to stolen tests, falsified records, vandalism of library materials, and breach of computer security.
       
    4. Other forms of intellectual dishonesty distorting such as knowingly furnishing false information, or omitting data that would provide the necessary and required information to the Registrar, the instructor/teacher, University and College offices for any academically related purposes.

    6. Misuse of or tampering with the University's electrical system, telephone service, fire safety equipment, and other security devices.
       
    7. Unauthorized entry into any University building, office or private property within the campus.
       
    8. Assault, physical or verbal abuse, intimidation, threats, harassment, coercion, or other conduct that threaten or endanger the health or safety of any person.
       
    9. Malicious dissemination of false reports, threats of fire, explosion or other emergency.

CCS Guidelines

  1. On Excused Absences
     
      An absence is only considered excused when supported by valid documents. Below are the possible reasons and the corresponding guidelines to be satisfied before an absence can be considered valid.
       
    1. When a student gets sick, the following should be presented:
       
      • An excuse letter duly signed by the parents or guardian;
         
      • A medical certificate issued by the University doctor to support the excuse.
         
      2. Medical certificates not coming from the University doctor is unacceptable, except those coming from specialists (e.g. for eye problems, the certificate must come from an EENT specialist).
       
    2. If the illness is minor and a medical certificate is not possible, the Guidance Counselor/Facilitator is given the task to determine the validity of the absence.
       
    3. When a student leaves for home to attend some personal/important matters, the student must secure an excuse letter signed by the parents/guardian and the Guidance Counselor stating the reason(s);
       
      4. Note: Documents referred to in #1 - #3 of this section must be presented to the faculty concerned signed by the Guidance Counselor/Facilitator within the week the student returns to school, otherwise the absence automatically becomes unexcused.
       
    4. When a student is sent to represent the University, he/she should:
       
      • secure in advance the excuse letter signed by the person in charge of the said event;
         
      • present the excuse letter signed by the Guidance Counselor/Facilitator to the faculty concerned before leaving the University.
         
    5. When a student is required to appear before a public or private office, the student must:
       
      • secure an excuse letter signed by the parents / guardian and the Guidance Counselor/Facilitator stating the reason;
         
      • submit supporting document/s from the office that requires his/her presence;
         
      • present the excuse letter to the faculty concerned before his/her scheduled appearance in the said office.
         
    6. Students who may have mental or physical health issues must secure a psychiatric or health report from their psychiatrist or medical doctor. Those who have special cases/issues/concerns that are highly confidential must secure a note from the Guidance Counselor.
       
  2. On Make-up Examinations
     
    1. It is the student's responsibility to take the initiative in approaching the faculty in taking make-up examinations.
       
    2. A student will only be allowed to take make-up examinations for major exams (e.g. prelim, midterm and final) provided that the absence is excused (see section on excused absences for guidelines.
       
    3. Make-up exams for quizzes, long examinations, hands-on exams and hands-on exercises are faculty's prerogative.
       
    4. If the date of the examination is the same as the date a student is advised by a physician to return to class, the student may opt to postpone taking the exam.
       
    5. It is the faculty's discretion to set the make-up examination schedule.
       
  3. On Attendance
     
    1. Three (3) unexcused late arrivals (15 minutes after the scheduled time a course begins) is equivalent to 1 unexcused absence.
       
    2. Three (3) consecutive unexcused absences will mean automatic dropping of the student from the class and a grade of “F”.
       
    3. Only three (3) unexcused and seven (7) excused absences are allowed for the entire semester. Penalty is the same as in #2 of this section.
       
  4. On Other Common Matters
     
    1. A student who has a grade of INC on any course has to complete the requirements of the same within one semester after the semester the course was taken, otherwise the grade will automatically become “F”.
       
    2. Any student caught cheating in a major examination (prelim, mid-term and finals) will automatically be given a grade of “F” on the course. For other exams / quizzes, the student will automatically be given a grade of “F” in that particular exam / quiz.
       
    3. All students must take major examinations as set by the College.
       
    4. Regarding prerequisite courses: If and when a student, due to an exceptional situation, has to take a prerequisite course simultaneously with a course requiring such a prerequisite, a failure in the prerequisite would mean no credit also for the course which required it, even if he/she passes the latter.
       
    5. Students of the College of Computer Studies (CCS) are required to attend all regular religious and academic convocations and assemblies. This policy also applies to all other special convocations/lectures/activities sponsored by the College and the University which are considered vital to the total development of the students. Students who are absent from the foregoing activities will be coursed for sanction/penalties as may be decided by the College and/or Confederates Student Council. College Activities may include but are not limited to: First Year Class Orientation, CCS Freshmen Orientation, All University Convocation, University Christian Life Emphasis Month (UCLEM), Orientation for Academically Challenged Students, CCS Founder’s Day, Juniors Team Building, Juniors BESS, College Days, Job Fair, Seniors Day, among others.
       
    6. Overload is only allowed during the last semester period of schooling.
       
  5. On College Clearance
     
    • The College clearance is a requirement before a student gets his/her proposed list (a list of courses to be taken for the incoming semester). This clearance needs to be signed by the Confederates (CCS Student Council), Guidance Counselor, IRC Librarian, Department Chairperson and the Dean.
       
  6. On Graduation Requirements
     
    1. On Thesis/Capstone Project Requirement.
       
      2. No student is allowed to enroll in this course if he/she is not a candidate for graduation in March or summer of the current school year. A student will not be allowed to graduate if he/she fails to completely submit all requirements (hardbound of the manuscript and softcopy of the program).

CCS Awards Guidelines

  1. Rationale
     
    • The rationale of the CCS Awards is to recognize students who best epitomize Silliman University's quality Christian education of academic excellence and student leadership.
       
  2. Dean's List Award
     
    • This award is given to regular students, regardless of year level, who have achieved and maintained a CQPA of at least 3.25.
       
  3. Highest QPA Award
     
    • This award is given to regular students, regardless of year level, who have achieved and maintained a CQPA of at least 3.25.
       
  4. Student/s of the Year Award
     
    • The Student/s of the Year Award is divided into four categories:
       
      • Freshman Student/s of the Year
         
      • Sophomore Student/s of the Year
         
      • Junior Student/s of the Year
         
      • Senior Student/s of the Year
         
    • Criteria:
       
    • Service 25%
      Leadership 25%
      Scholarship 25%
      Talent 25%  
      TOTAL 100%

       
    • Freshman Student/s of the Year
      • The student must meet the residency requirement of at least one semester of the entire freshman year.
         
    • Sophomore Student/s of the Year
      • The student must meet the residency requirement of a full sophomore year and at least one semester.
         
    • Junior Student/s of the Year
      • The student must meet the residency requirement of a full junior year and at least 2 semesters.
         
    • Senior Student/s of the Year
      • The student must meet the residency requirement of at least two years including the senior year.
         
  5. Talent Awards
     
    • These awards will be given to students with outstanding talents, outside of the academic discipline, and who have generously shared them in enriching campus/College and/or community life.
       
    • The Talent Awards are categorized into the following distinctions:
       
      • Excellence in Literacy Arts
         
      • Excellence in Performing Arts
         
      • Excellence in Visual Arts
         
      • Excellence in Athletics
         
    • Criteria:
       
    • Talent 50%
      Scholarship 25%
      Leadership & Service 25%  
      TOTAL 100%

       
    • A residency of at least one year or two semesters including the current school year is required. Nominees are subject for a panel interview, if deemed necessary by the Committee. Scholarship should be based on the CQPA of at least 2.50 or better, with no grades of “F”, “WF” or “INC”. Activities should be based upon the past school year.
       
  6. Leadership and Service Awards
     
    • These awards will be given to students of any year level as selected by the CCS Confederates Student Council adviser.
       
  7. Laboratory Assistant of the Year Award
     
    • This award will be given to the most deserving CCS laboratory assistant (student assistant) as unanimously selected by the CCS Laboratory Assistants Adviser and the faculty.
       
  8. Special Award
     
    • This award will be given to the most deserving CCS laboratory assistant (student assistant) as unanimously selected by the CCS Laboratory Assistants Adviser and the faculty.
       
    • Selection process:
       
      1. Each department chairperson shall elect a member from his/her department to be a member of the committee.
         
      2. Faculty members will nominate students per category for the Leadership Award, and Laboratory Assistant Award.
         
    • The nominated students will be evaluated by a committee composed of an appointed Chairperson and appointed representatives from the 3 Departments and the Guidance Counselor/Facilitator. Only the representatives from the 3 Departments will be able to vote in any situation that requires a vote.
